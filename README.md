Sales Alone Can Be the Biggest Deception in Supply Chain!
Recently, while working on a supply chain and sales data analysis project using SQL, I uncovered insights that demonstrate how "big numbers" can mask operational and financial disasters if not examined through an analyst's lens.

The company achieved total sales of $39,627 with a net profit of $2,592, reflecting a very healthy profit margin of 15.3%. From an initial reading, there were no stagnant products—everything was selling! However, when I used SQL to dive deeper into operations, logistics, and segments, several critical issues were revealed:

The Shipping Mode Crisis (Standard Class): The Standard Class shipping method is causing a major bottleneck. It accumulated a total of 41 days of delivery delays, which directly cost the company a financial loss of $192.02.

Geographic Logistics Bottleneck: Central America emerged as a clear bottleneck in the supply chain, accounting for 20 days of delays in shipping and delivery operations on its own.

The Home Office Segment Illusion: When analyzing customer segments, the Home Office sector recorded the highest average sales value. Paradoxically, its total revenue was the lowest. SQL analysis showed that the average product price in this segment is exceptionally high, reaching up to $162 per item. While this inflates the average order value, the actual volume of orders is low and requires strategic activation.

The Best-Selling Product Puzzle and Pricing Strategy: The highest-moving product category across the entire company is Cleats, and the single best-selling item is the "Field & Stream Sportsman 16 Gun Fire Safe." The catch? Despite its massive sales volume, this specific product is generating a negative net profit. This is because the average discount granted on it is an astronomical 39.41%, completely wiping out its profit margin.

Action Plan and Recommendations for Management
Logistics: Restructure the Standard Class shipping system and prioritize shipments heading to Central America to eliminate delivery delays.

Finance & Marketing: Cap the maximum discount allowed on the Field & Stream product at 15% to guarantee its profitability. Simultaneously, launch targeted marketing campaigns for the Home Office segment to increase order volume and capitalize on its high average product prices.

Supply chain data is not just about tables and code; it dictates critical decisions that protect business profitability.
